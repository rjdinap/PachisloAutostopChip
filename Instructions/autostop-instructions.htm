Modchip v1.1 Instructions

 

 

Definitions:

 

 

Button press: press the button for 1-2 seconds. Whenever we say 'press' a button, always assume 1-2 seconds.

 

Button tap: a quick press of a button, less than 1 second

 

Press and hold: several functions will require you to press and hold one button for 2 seconds. While continuing to hold the first button, tap the second button.

 

 

 

Quick use instructions:

 

 

When you first power up your machine, the chip is in a 'dormant' state, waiting for input.

 

To enable autostop mode, press the left reel stop button. Autostop mode will now be enabled until you disable it. To disable autostop mode, press the left reel stop button again. Autostop mode will now be disabled until you re-enable it. You can use this feature at any time, as many times as you wish. You can even enable / disable autostop mode while the reels are spinning (although pressing the left reel stop button while the reels are spinning will cause that reel to stop.)

 

To load the machine with 50 credits, press the right reel stop button. This will only work if the machine is expecting coin input. For example, if the reels are spinning and you press the right reel stop button, it will not load credits.

 

 

 

Additional functionality:

 

 

To disable autostop mode until a reboot, press and hold the left button, then tap the middle button.

 

To disable the credit add function until a reboot, press and hold the right button, then tap the middle button.

 

To put the machine into coin credit calibration mode, press the middle button.

 

To increase the time between reel stops in autostop mode, press and hold the middle button, then tap the left button.

 

To decrease the time between reel stops in autostop mode, press and hold the middle button, then tap the right button.

 

To enable / disable reel randomization mode, press and hold the left button, then tap the right button.

 

To disable autostop for 1 round only, hold down the game start lever for 2-3 seconds.

 

 

 

Reel Randomization

 

 

When you first enable autostop mode, reels will stop in a left to right order. You can have the reels stop in a random order by pressing the left button, then tapping the right button. Repeating this command will make the reels stop in a left to right order again.

 

 

 

Details on increasing / decreasing reel timing

 

 

The default timing for reel stops is approximately 1/2 of a second. (The first reel will stop, wait 1/2 second, stop the second reel, wait 1/2 second, stop the 3rd reel.)

 

By pressing the middle button and then tapping the left/right buttons, you increase or decrease the reel stop timing by 1/10 of a second.

For example, you press and hold the middle button, then tap the left button 3 times. The chip will now wait 8/10 of a second between stopping reels. The minimum reel stop time is 1/3 second. There is no maximum.

 

Note: If you press and hold the middle button and then release it without tapping the right or left button, the machine will enter calibrate mode.

 

 

 

Calibrate mode:

 

 

Normally, as long as you wired up the coin sensors correctly, you merely press the right reel stop button to add credits. There may be a few models however, in which the default timing that the modchip sends for coin pulses is incorrect. This would cause the machine to register a coin error, instead of adding credits. We haven't encountered any machines where this happens yet, but we've planned for it!

 

Pressing the middle button will make the machine enter calibrate mode. After entering calibrate mode, the chip will wait for you to insert a coin into the coin slot. It will measure the length of time the coin takes to pass the coin sensors, and use this timing when simulating coin input. This has the advantage of guaranteeing our chip will work with almost any coin mech, as long as the coin mech has 2 coin sensors.

 

Calibration has the added advantage of making the add credit routine much faster. Before calibration, adding 50 credits will take about 6 seconds. After calibration, adding 50 credits usually only takes around 3 seconds.

 

Note: the only way the chip will exit calibrate mode is to insert a coin, or power the machine off.

 

Note: There are NO KNOWN machines that NEED this feature yet. It is merely to 'future proof' the chip, or allow faster coin input.

 

 

 

Pin 4 wiring

 

 

Occasionally, you will press the start button or game lever handle and notice that the machine does not respond. This is pretty rare, but it does happen. The reason that this happens is because the autostop chip is pulsing out a reelstop command at the exact same time you are pressing the start button or game lever handle.

If you want to eliminate the possbility of a non-response, you can wire pin 4. To understand why this works, we can look at 2 possible modes that the chip operates in:

 

 

- With pin 4 wired

 

1) The chip looks for command input (add credits, toggle autostop mode, etc)

2) If it has a command, it goes and completes that routine

3) If the reels are reels spinning (based on pin4 input), it:

       a) waits 3 seconds

       b) it pulses out the reel stop signals, based on default timing or your customized timing

4) goes back to step 1 - looking for input

 

 

 

- Without pin 4 wired

 

1) The chip looks for command input

2) If it has a command, it goes and completes that routine

3) It pulses out the reel stop signals, based on default timing, or your customized timing

4) goes back to step 1 - looking for input

 

 

The difference between these 2 modes is in step three. If pin 4 is wired, it only pulses out reel stop commands after it sees the reels spinning. If pin 4 is not wired, it is constantly pulsing out reel stop signals. The chip is smart; it automatically knows if pin 4 is wired!

 

Wiring pin 4 will also have the advantage of forcing the left reel to stop first (if reel randomization is off). If pin 4 is not wired, the reels will stop in a left to right order, but since the machine is constantly pulsing out reel stop signals it may stop the middle or right reel first.

 

 

 

Other Notes:

 

The reel stop boards seem to hold power for quite a while (up to ~10 seconds) after the machine is powered off. Even though you have may have powered off the pachislo machine, the chip still executes code for a while. If you power the machine off and then on quickly, the chip will be in the same mode it was before the machine was powered off (ie; if autostop mode was on, it will still be on). You need to power the machine off for at least 5-10 seconds to make sure the autostop chip starts fresh.
