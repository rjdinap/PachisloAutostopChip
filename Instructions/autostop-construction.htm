The Pachislo USA, Inc. Super Autostop Modchip constuction page - 1

Please read the disclaimer at the end of this document. If you don't agree with the disclaimer, you are not entitled to perform this modification.

What you need:

low wattage soldering iron / solder

hot glue gun

thin wire (wirewrap wire, available from Radio Shack works great)

our socketed autostop chip, shown below

you may need a multimeter if installing the credit add function or optional 8th wire on pin 4

 

 

Socketed autostop chip:

Pin 1 -  +5v

Pin 2 - coin mod sensor 1

Pin 3 - coin mod sensor 2

Pin 4 - optional  - game start lever

Pin 5 - reel 3 stop

Pin 6 - reel 2 stop

Pin 7 - reel 1 stop

Pin 8 -  ground

 

Note the notch (indentation) on the top of the chip. The notch must face pins 1/8. If you put the chip in backwards, you risk damaging the chip.

 

Find the wiring points for board. Click on the picture of your board style to enlarge the picture.

 

Detailed instructions:

1. Remove the chip from the socket. You can insert a small screwdriver between the chip and the socket and gently rock it from side to side. Do not solder to the socket with the chip inserted - you take the risk of damaging the pic chip from heat.

2. Cut 5 wires (7 if wiring for the 50 credit mod). 3-4" for each wire is more than long enough. Strip 1/16 - 1/8" insulation off both ends of the wire. Wirewrap wire is highly recommended because it is very thin and flexible.

2. Solder one end of the wires to the socket on pins 1,5,6,7,8. If you are wiring up the 50 credit coin mod, you can also wire pins 2 and 3. Wire 4 is optional. Read the instruction manual to find out more about the start game lever. A low wattage soldering iron (~30 watts) is highly recommended.

3. Use hot glue to attach the socket to the reel stop board. Be careful not to get glue in the metal socket pins. The glue holds best if you cover all 4 sides of the socket.

4. Solder the wires from the socket to the reel stop board. If you have used pins 2,3 and 4, just tape the the ends for now (make sure that the exposed wires aren't touching each other)

5. Insert the pic chip, observing that notch in the chip is facing where you wired pins 1 and 8.

Your finished auto-stop board should look similar to this (This is our prototype board. Hopefully, you used shorter wires. This picture does not show the optional wire on pin 4 of the chip.)
